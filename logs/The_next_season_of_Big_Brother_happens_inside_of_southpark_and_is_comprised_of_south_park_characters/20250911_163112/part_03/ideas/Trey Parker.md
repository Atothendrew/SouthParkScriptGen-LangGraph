### Key Creative Choices

1. **“Please” as death** – We turn a harmless politeness marker into lethal punishment to lampoon how social media “filters” and algorithmic moderation can over‑reach.  
2. **Squeaky duck narrator** – Keeps the tone irreverent and provides a vehicle for absurd asides that reference pop‑culture (e.g., “Duck Dodgers” jokes).  
3. **Flood & hologram glitch** – Classic slapstick meets satire: a flood demonstrates the fragility of Randy’s empire, while the hologram shows the absurdity of over‑idealized celebrity.  
4. **Musical parody** – Cartman’s “Wet & Wild” song gives a quick, catchy break that also underscores the absurdity of communal chaos.  
5. **Kyle’s rebirth** – Resurrecting Kyle underscores that the show’s satire isn’t about politeness but about how technology punishes nuance.  
6. **Portal to the “Real World”** – Sets up an open‑ended conflict for the next part: is it better to live unmonitored or continue watching reality?  

The outline stays true to South Park’s irreverent, character‑driven humor while escalating from a simple classroom gag into a full‑blown tech dystopia, ending with a twisted moral learning moment and a hook for the next episode.
