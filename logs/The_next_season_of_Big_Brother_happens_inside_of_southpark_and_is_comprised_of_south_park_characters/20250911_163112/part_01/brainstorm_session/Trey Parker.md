# Trey Parker Brainstorm Session

**South Park Episode Outline – “Big Brother? Big Frustration!”**

*Written by Trey Parker & Matt Stone, with a comedic‑style “brain‑storm” from Bill Hader.*

---

## 1. PREMISE & SATIRICAL TARGETS

| Target | Satirical Angle |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Surveillance Culture** | Every corner of the “Big Brother” house is a camera; even the town’s paparazzi are glued to the live feed. |
| **Algorithmic Judgment** | A “smart AI” gives random “sincerity scores”; the town votes based on a ridiculous spreadsheet. |
| **Reality‑TV Commodification** | The show sells “Kyle’s Moral Lessons” as a pay‑per‑view special, Randy sells canned air‑fresheners. |
| **Political Polarization** | The “Debate” segment devolves into a caricature of real‑world punditry. |
| **Social‑Media Fandoms** | Hashtags, chat‑bots, and a “Trending” feed that never changes. |
| **Control & Ego** | Randy’s compulsion to micromanage mirrors the absurdity of “big‑brother” paranoia. |

---

## 2. STRUCTURE (22 min)

| Act | Beats & Key Scenes |
|-----|--------------------|
| **Act 1 – The Big Brother Invasion** | 1. *Big Brother* crew rolls into town; Randy is announced as the unseen host (a parody of “Unseen” reality‑TV hosts). <br>2. Kids are recruited to live in the massive house for a $100,000 prize; cameras installed everywhere. <br>3. Randy’s first “rule‑making” scene: he decrees that every greeting must be theatrical, and he starts building a “control‑center” in the basement. |
| **Act 2 – Control & Chaos** | 1. *Control‑Center Catastrophe*: Randy’s gadgets backfire (talking mirror, laser‑fridge), sprinklers flood the living room. <br>2. *First Challenge – Shelter Build*: Kids must build a shelter using lawn furniture; Kyle’s earnestness is misread by the AI, he gets “low sincerity.” <br>3. *Algorithmic Judgment*: The AI’s score sheet is a random number generator; the town votes based on it. <br>4. *Cartman’s Big‑Brother Drama*: Cartman tries to “control” votes with a “censor” AI that flips back on him. <br>5. *Randy’s Remote Disaster*: He pulls out a dumbbell‑remote, yells “BRAIN!” and the dumbbell ricochets into the chandelier. |
| **Act 3 – The Musical Showdown** | 1. *The Great Debate*: Kids debate a current issue while the camera crew livestreams; Chef gives a heartfelt speech about community. <br>2. *The Musical “Song of Truth”*: Each kid performs a parody song; Randy’s song (“I’m the King”) is over‑the‑top. <br>3. *The Real Big Brother Reveal*: The camera that has been “watching” turns out to be a rubber duck; the AI is actually a school‑board chatbot. <br>4. *Randy’s Epiphany*: The control‑center collapses; Randy admits he’s scared of being “forgotten.” |
| **Act 4 – Resolution & Moral** | 1. *Town’s Toast*: The kids raise a glass to Randy (“To the real big brother!”). <br>2. *Randy’s Hug*: Sharon gives him an awkward but genuine hug; Randy accepts he already has the “big brother” title. <br>3. *Post‑Show Wrap‑Up*: The crew leaves; a voice‑over says, “And that’s how we keep America entertained.” <br>4. *Closing Shot*: The house is empty, a billboard reads “Big Brother? No. Big Friends.” |

## 3. CHARACTER MOMENTS

| Character | Moment | Why It Works |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| **Randy** | Builds the control‑center, then collapses in front of everyone; finally says “I’m scared we’ll forget me.” | Satirizes the over‑complicating self‑importance of “big‑brother” figures. |
| **Cartman** | Tries to manipulate the AI, ends up being “censored.” | Classic Cartman drama turned into a reality‑TV parody. |
| **Kyle** | Speaks up about the AI’s randomness, later writes a school‑paper article. | Provides moral backbone and satirical commentary on algorithmic truth. |
| **Stan** | Wins the final vote, but is visibly drained; he chooses to donate part of his prize. | Highlights the cost of fame and prizes. |
| **Chef** | Gives a heartfelt speech in the debate, “We’re all family.” | Shows the show’s hidden heart. |
| **Elon Musk** (impersonated) | Appears as a “tech‑savvy sidekick” who explains Randy’s gadgets. | Adds tech satire and modern‑culture reference. |
| **Joe Biden** (impersonated) | Host of the show, uses catch‑phrases (“Let me tell you something!”). | Parody of political hosts. |
| **Sharon** | Gives Randy the awkward hug that ends the episode. | Provides emotional payoff. |

## 4. COMEDY‑SCRIPTS (selected scenes)

**Scene: Randy’s Control‑Center Catastrophe**

```
INT. RUNDOWN BASEMENT – DAY

Randy is surrounded by blinking lights, a talking mirror (“Hey Randy, you look *so* intense.”), and a laser‑guided fridge that shoots out frozen pizza.

RANDY
(voice‑over)
So, basically… I’m the *big* brother here.

He pulls a giant remote that’s actually a dumbbell. He yells:

RANDY
BRAIN! (throws the dumbbell)

The dumbbell ricochets, hits a chandelier, and triggers an automated sprinkler system. Water floods the basement.

RICKY (the Smart Mirror)
(voice‑over, deadpan)
You’re welcome for the shower.

Randy freezes. The mirror flickers.

MIRROR
(voice‑over)
You need a *new* control center, Randy. Or maybe just… stop.

Randy looks at the remote as if it’s a cursed object.
```

**Scene: The AI “Sincerity” Score**

```
INT. LIVING ROOM – NIGHT

The kids are watching a live feed on a giant screen labeled “Sincerity Score: ___/10”.

AI (a hologram, glitchy voice)
Kyle Broflovski—Sincerity Score: 3.14159/10.

The kids gasp; the town begins voting. The score is a random number generator; it keeps flipping.

KYLE
(angry)
This is ridiculous! It’s not a real score!

The AI just flashes “ERROR”.

STAN
Maybe it’s measuring something else—like the amount of *air* you’re breathing.

The town votes anyway, chanting “Vote for Kyle!” because they love his earnestness.
```

**Scene: The Musical “Song of Truth”**

```
INT. STAGE – NIGHT

A makeshift stage, bright lights and a “DJ” (Chef) spinning beats.

KARTMAN
(raps)
I’m the best, I’ll make you see—this is my reality!

STAN
(sings)
I’ve seen the world from here, and I’m tired of fear.

KYLE
(sings softly)
Let’s just be honest—no more lies, no more fakes.

RANDY
(awkwardly)
I’m the *king*, I’m the *big*—wait, what?

Chef flips a vinyl and drops a beat. The crowd goes wild.

VOICE‑OVER (Biden impersonation)
Let me tell you something: this is the real show.
```

## 5. CREATIVE CHOICES & Reasoning

| Choice | Why It Works |
|--------|--------------|
| **Big Brother as a “Big Brother” show** | Directly satirizes modern reality TV and surveillance. |
| **Randy’s control‑center as a physical gag** | Provides tangible, visual humor and a clear escalation of his neurotic traits. |
| **AI as random number generator** | Highlights how algorithms can be arbitrary and absurd. |
| **Rubber‑duck reveal** | Classic South Park twist: the “big brother” isn’t a serious entity but a silly prop, turning satire into a punchline. |
| **Inclusion of impersonations (Elon Musk, Joe Biden)** | Keeps the show topical and allows for meta‑commentary on tech & politics. |
| **Musical “Song of Truth”** | Signature South Park element that lets characters showcase their personalities in song. |
| **Emotional payoff (Randy’s hug, Sharon)** | Gives depth to a comedic episode; audiences recognize the “big‑brother” is actually just a caring adult. |
| **Ending with a billboard** | Visual closure that reinforces the moral and satirical message. |

## 7. FINAL OUTLINE

**Title:** *“Big Brother? Big Frustration!”*  
**Tagline:** “When the town’s biggest problem is… who’s watching?”

- **Beginning:** The town welcomes a *Big Brother* reality‑TV show. Randy becomes the unseen host, and the kids are forced to live in a house full of cameras.
- **Middle:** Randy’s obsessive control‑center causes chaos, the AI gives absurd scores, and Cartman tries to manipulate the outcome. The kids face challenges that parody real‑world reality TV tropes.
- **Climax:** A musical showdown reveals the “real” Big Brother is a rubber duck. Randy’s control‑center collapses, exposing his fear of being forgotten.
- **End:** The town toasts Randy as the real big brother, he accepts his role, and the episode closes with a billboard that says *“Big Brother? No. Big Friends.”*

**Tone:** Irreverent, satirical, character‑driven; the episode is packed with physical gags (remote‑dumbbell, sprinkler flood), musical numbers, and a heartfelt twist that gives the show its signature blend of outrageous comedy and genuine insight.
