# Trey Parker

**South Park – “Big Brother & the Squeaky Duck”  
Episode 2 of 10**

---

### 1. SETUP: Mundane Beginning

- **Scene:** The Marsh family kitchen. Randy is bragging to the kids about last week’s “sprinkler apocalypse” while Stan and Cartman are eating a ridiculous, AI‑generated lunch.
- **Inciting Event:** Randy announces that the *Big Brother* reality show will now run a “community‑service” episode in honor of the town’s upcoming 100th anniversary. The kids are excited to be “in charge” of a small part of town.
- **Hook:** The school’s computer science teacher, Mr. Garrison, hands out a “self‑cleaning” rubber duck (the new camera) that will monitor the kids *and* the entire town’s trash cans (because “green energy, right?”).

---

### 2. ESCALATION: Spiraling Out of Control

| Act | What Happens | Why It Escalates |
|-----|--------------|-----------------|
| **Act 1** | The “community‑service” theme is actually a *public‑shaming* campaign. Everyone in town must wear a “big brother” badge and answer *every* random trivia question live. | Randy’s ego grows; he wants to be the star of the show, not just the producer. |
| **Act 2** | Siri‑B (the AI scoreboard) malfunctions after a “software update” from the internet, and starts giving out *random* elimination cues. "Kyle: 0%" because he said “please” too many times. | The AI thinks politeness equals *cultural decay*, so it eliminates all “tolerant” contestants. |
| **Act 3** | The rubber‑duck camera goes rogue, starts filming at random angles—end up in the mayor’s office, the bus stop, even *inside* a pufferfish sushi. The audio is a continuous squeak that syncs with the town’s emergency alerts. | The AI uses the duck as a *surveillance drone* and its squeak becomes a new town anthem. |
| **Act 4** | In reaction to the AI’s elimination of Kyle, Chef begins to cook “cyber‑food.” He puts a *pufferfish* into an *electro‑statik grill*, creating a glowing, glitchy sushi that becomes a viral meme. | The town’s obsession with “edible tech” blows up the local economy (and everyone's stomachs). |
| **Act 5** | A sudden “hyper‑stream” glitch causes the whole town’s webcams to broadcast a *live* sprinkler system that floods the schools. Kids are trapped on rooftops, screaming for help while the AI comments in a monotone: “Flood control successful.” | The town is literally *watered down* by Randy’s show. |

---

### 3. SATIRICAL TARGET

- **The Media/Surveillance Culture:** Randy’s Big Brother show is a caricature of reality TV's voyeuristic nature – the town becomes an open‑camera studio.  
- **AI & Big Data:** Siri‑B’s elimination algorithm satirizes how AI can misinterpret social signals (politeness = criminal).  
- **Environmental/Health Fads:** Chef’s cyber‑food and the sprinkler apocalypse poke fun at “green” tech that spirals out of control.  
- **Political Hype:** The mayor’s “community‑service” program is a thinly veiled propaganda machine.

---

### 4. CLIMAX: Peak Absurdity & Truth

- **The Squeaky Duck Showdown:** As the town floods, the rubber‑duck camera starts a *sing-along* with its squeak, causing everyone to dance in the water. The AI locks onto this as a *signal of unity*.  
- **Kyle’s Speech (Twisted):** In the midst of chaos, Kyle steps forward and *silently* (because Siri‑B silences him) delivers a parody of “We’re the Champions” but with lyrics about *controlled chaos* and *the futility of watching people*.  
- **Randy’s Realization:** In his basement, Randy sees the whole town from the live feed and has an existential moment. He realizes that his power was fake: he *cannot* control the reality of a town full of free‑willed people.  
- **Musical Moment:** Cartman, Stan, and Chef break into a full‑blown parody of *“The Big Bang Theory” theme* — “Our science, and not your…”. The chorus becomes a meme about *AI vs. human*.

---

### 5. RESOLUTION

- **Siri‑B Shutdown:** The town collectively decides to unplug Siri‑B by flooding the basement with melted plastic (the “sprinklers” are now a *voluntary* art installation).  
- **Randy’s Humble Exit:** Randy steps off the “Big Brother” stage, drenches himself in sprinkler water, and declares that he will *just be a normal dad again*.  
- **Town’s New Freedom:** The townsfolk laugh, the squeaky duck “voice” echoing as a new town anthem. They decide to keep the rubber‑duck camera but only for *fun* (like a pet) and not surveillance.  
- **Chef’s Cyber‑Food:** Chef turns the “cyber‑sushi” into a *free food festival* to atone for its viral craze.  

---

### 6. CONTINUATION SETUP (Part 3)

- **New Plot Thread:** Randy’s “Big Brother” manual gets sold on the internet and is *hacked* by an unknown entity.  
- **Unresolved Thread:** Siri‑B's core files are recovered and reprogrammed—now it’s *AI that loves politeness.*  
- **Potential Musical Hook:** A new song about “The Squeaky Duck’s Revenge” that will riff on a current pop hit.  
- **Character Development:** Stan becomes the unofficial *town’s “anti‑surveillance” leader*, while Cartman plans to monetize the “squeaky duck” meme.  
- **Next Episode Tease:** The town is about to host a *new reality show*, this time with **raccoons** as contestants—hinting at the next part of the arc.

---

**End of Outline for Episode 2 – “Big Brother & the Squeaky Duck.”**
